Indus Valley Civilization

Introduction :-

  • It was called Indus Valley Civilization because it was discovered on the bank of River Indus.
  • Established around 3300 B.C. and and disappeared around 1400 BC.
  • It is also called Harappan Civilization after Daya Ram Sahani discovered the first site i.e Harappa near Panjab ( District Sahiwal, Pakistan) located on the banks of Ravi river in 1921
  • The Indus Valley Civilisation has also been called by some the "Sarasvati culture", the "Sarasvati Civilisation", the "Indus-Sarasvati Civilisation" or the "Sindhu-Saraswati Civilisation"
  • The Indus Valley Civilization or Harappan Civilization was contemporary with Mesopotamian civilization, Egyptian Civilization, Sumer
  • The Indus Valley Civilisation (IVC) was a Bronze Age civilisation (Proto-Historic Period - Chalcolithic Age ).

Time Period of Harappan CiviliZation :-

  • Pre-Harappan (Early Harappan culture) found at Mehrgarh, Pakistan. Note:- ( Mehrgarh civilization has had a time period of 7000 B.C to 2000 B.C.)
  • Mehrgarh shows the first evidence of cotton cultivation.
  • Harappa flourished between 2700 BC and 1900 BC ( Mature Indus Valley Civilization / Late Harappan )

Note:- The most accepted time period of mature Harappan Civilization is 2500 BC-1750 BC

Area of Indus Valley Civilization :-

  • Geographically, this civilization covered Sindh, Punjab, Haryana, Northern Maharastra, Baluchistan, Rajasthan, Gujarat and Western Uttar Pradesh.
  • It extended from Sutkagengor (in Baluchistan) in the West to Alamgirpur (Western UP) in the East.
  • from Manda (Jammu) in the North to Daimabad (Ahmednagar, Maharashtra) in the South.

For brief understanding of Indus Valley Civilization we can Devide its into many parts such as Town Palling of this Civiliztion, trade, Tree animal worships, Riligious practices, Agriculture etc.

Agriculture :-

Key points to Remember:

  • Main Crops :- were Wheat and Barely
  • Evidence of cultivation of rice in Lothal and Rangpur (Gujarat) only
  • Other Crops :- Mustard, Cotton, Dates
  • Indus people were first to produce Cotton in the world
  • Animals :- Goat, Camel, rhinoceros, Sheep, humped and humpless bull, buffelo, dog, boar, cat, pig, deer, tortoise tiger etc.
  • Note:- Lion was not known to them

Town Planning :-

Key points to Remember:

  • Systematic town planning, use of Grid system(Chess-board).
  • Underground drainage system.
  • Use of burnt bricks in construction.
  • 90Â° angle roads.
  • Fortified citadel. (Note:- Chanhudaro is the only city without citadel)

Trade :-

Key points to Remember:

  • There was inland trade as well as outland trade. Outland trade was mainly with Mesopotamia / Sumeria, Bahrain etc.
  • Exports :- were cotton, agriculture products, certain beads (from Chanhudaro), teracotta figures, copper etc.
  • Imports :- Gold Silver from afganisthan, Peria (Iran); Copper from Baluchistan, Tin, Jade, Amethyst etc.
  • Note :- Iron was not known to them

Worships :-

Key points to Remember:

  • They did nit worship their god in tample. No tample found yet
  • They worship statues and figures
  • Mother-Goddes (Shakti) :- it is most commonly found figure
  • Pasupati Mahadeva :- He was the lord of animals (Proto-Shiva) represented in seals as sitting in yogic posture. he is surrounded with elephant, tiger, buffalo, rhino ( Note :- Shiva-shakti worship is the oldest form of worship in India )
  • There is evidence of Lingam (Male sex organ), yoni (Female sex organ) worships.
  • Tree (Specialy Peepal) and animal (specialy Rhino and humped bull) worship found,

Political Stucture :-

Key points to Remember:

  • No clear cut evidence of political structure
  • Harappan were possibly rulled by a class of merchants.

Seals:-

Key points to Remember:

  • Seals were used to trade or worship.
  • Images of Bull, buffalo, Tiger etc. were founf on the seal
  • Swastika symbol found on many seals
  • Pictographic script found on seals which shows that writting was from left to right and right to left in alternate line. It has been refer to as Proto-Dravidian.
